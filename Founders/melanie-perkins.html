The Insight

While teaching design software, Melanie Perkins saw students struggle with tools that were powerful but unnecessarily difficult. The opportunity was not merely better software. It was making visual communication approachable for people without formal design training.

The strongest product ideas often begin as repeated customer frustration.

02 · Analysis

Starting Narrow

Before Canva, Perkins and Cliff Obrecht built Fusion Books, an online school yearbook tool. This narrower market allowed them to test collaborative browser-based design, printing workflows, and customer support before attempting a global platform.

03 · Analysis

Patient Fundraising

The founders faced repeated investor rejection. Instead of abandoning the vision, they improved the pitch, built credibility through Fusion Books, and developed relationships over several years. Persistence mattered, but so did continuously reducing the perceived risk.

04 · Analysis

Growth System

Canva combined a generous free product, templates that shortened time-to-value, collaboration loops, and paid upgrades for advanced needs. Each completed design could introduce another person to the product, allowing usage itself to support distribution.

05 · Analysis

Founder Lesson

Big visions become more believable when founders prove one difficult assumption at a time. Start with a constrained problem, build evidence, and preserve the ambition while changing the path.
